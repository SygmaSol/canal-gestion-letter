How We Work

Where we differ from most leak detection companies -- and there are very few operating here in Lanzarote -- is in the breadth of technology we use and the investment we have made in it. Most operators rely on one or two pieces of equipment. We believe our accuracy comes from two things: using multiple technologies in combination to cross-confirm findings, and the significant investment we have made in pipe location equipment specifically.

That second point matters more than it might seem. Before you can find a leak, you need to know exactly where the pipe runs. On a property with tiled floors, a garden, and buried infrastructure that may have been modified over the years, that is not always obvious. We carry specialist pipe location equipment that allows us to map the pipe route accurately before any detection work begins. Locating the pipe correctly is the foundation of everything else -- it is what allows us to place other instruments in the right position, and it is what makes the difference between pinpointing a leak and guessing.

From there, we deploy a range of non-destructive technologies -- acoustic correlators, ground-penetrating radar, CCTV pipe inspection cameras, and tracer gas detection -- using whichever combination the situation calls for, and cross-referencing the results before we commit to a location. We have over £80,000 of specialist equipment on the island. We locate the source to within centimetres before a single tile is lifted or a spade goes in the ground. The repair is targeted, the disruption is minimal, and the customer's property is left as we found it.

This matters in the context of bill reduction claims because the alternative -- calling a general plumber and letting them dig until they find the source -- can easily cost more than the reduction is worth. We have seen customers spend €1,500 on excavation work to recover €400 from a bill reduction. That is not a good outcome for anyone. With us, that situation does not arise.

Every job we complete is supported by documentation written entirely in Spanish: a full technical report detailing the method used, the exact location of the leak, and photographic evidence; a formal invoice; and a written repair guarantee. This is structured documentation, prepared to a professional standard, that any water company can assess clearly and without ambiguity.

LeakGuard: Protecting Lanzarote's Water

Beyond reactive leak detection, we want to share something we are building here that we believe aligns directly with Canal Gestión's interests.

Water is a finite and precious resource on this island in a way it simply is not on the mainland. Everyone who lives and works here understands that. It is part of why we started this company, and it is the thinking behind a system we have developed called LeakGuard.

LeakGuard is a proactive water monitoring network that we are rolling out across Lanzarote. Rather than waiting for a customer to receive a shocking bill and then call us, the system detects anomalies in consumption as they happen -- catching leaks early, before significant water has been lost and before damage has had time to spread. We already have a growing number of properties enrolled, and that base continues to expand.

The system is built around software we have developed entirely in-house. Property owners can log in and view their water consumption day by day and hour by hour. They can see exactly how much the garden irrigation is using, how much the pool is consuming, and whether any toilet is passing water overnight. It gives people a level of visibility over their own usage that most have never had before.

The effect of that visibility matters. Studies in water management have consistently shown that when people have direct access to their own consumption data, they become meaningfully more aware of how they use water -- and they act on it. They fix problems faster, they reduce wastage, and they take ownership of their impact. LeakGuard is designed to make that happen at scale across the island.

You can read more about LeakGuard at leakguardlanzarote.com.

We raise this because we believe it is relevant to how Canal Gestión might see us. We are not simply a company that fixes leaks when called. We are actively investing in the infrastructure and tools to reduce water loss across Lanzarote -- and the more customers trust us with their properties, the greater that impact becomes.
